Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This privacy policy applies to Private Journal and other offline applications developed by Muneer Hasan. This policy explains, plainly, what data the apps do and do not handle.

Data collection

The apps do not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Specifically:

  • The apps do not require an account, sign-in, or email address.
  • The apps do not connect to the internet or any server.
  • All data (such as journal entries) you write is stored only on your device, in a local database.
  • Your PIN/passwords are never stored in plain text — only a salted cryptographic hash is kept, on-device, using your device's secure storage (Android Keystore / iOS Keychain).
  • If you enable fingerprint/face unlock, that authentication is handled entirely by your device's operating system. The app never sees or stores your biometric data.
  • The apps do not use analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • The apps do not request access to your contacts, location, camera, microphone, or photos unless explicitly required for a core feature.

Data deletion

Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all associated data, since nothing is stored anywhere else. You can also manually delete all entries at any time from the app's Settings.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if a future version adds an optional backup feature), this document will be updated and the change will be described in the app's changelog.
